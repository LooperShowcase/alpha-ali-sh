    • Overview: Gorillas are the largest living primates, belonging to the family Hominidae, which also includes humans, chimpanzees, and orangutans. They are native to the forests of central sub-Saharan Africa. Gorillas are highly intelligent animals, known for their complex social structures and expressive behaviors.

    Size and Appearance: Gorillas are the biggest primates. Males are bigger than females and have a patch of silver hair on their backs, earning them the name "silverbacks." Diet: They mostly eat plants like leaves, stems, and fruits. Sometimes they eat insects too. Social Life: Gorillas live in groups called troops, usually led by a silverback male. They are social animals and communicate with sounds, gestures, and facial expressions. Habitat: They live in forests, including rainforests and mountain forests. Gorillas make nests from leaves and branches to sleep in every night. Conservation: Gorillas are endangered because of habitat loss, hunting, and diseases. Efforts are being made to protect them and their homes.
